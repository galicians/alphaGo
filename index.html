Idea to Product

You will be in our lab from 6:00-8:30, Monday to Thursday, for 12 weeks.
In return, you will end up with a working Minimum Viable Product (MVP) and all the knowledge and skills to transform any idea into a digital product and impress potential investors.
Our evening course will provide you with an environment of like-minded people who will help you to stay motivated and build the right habits.

The Skills you will learn

Architecture

Architecture:
You have an idea in mind. To transform that idea into a product, you will need to come up with a system architectural design. You will learn the thinking process to come up with your own MVP.

Development

Development:
You will transform your architecture design into code. You will learn JavaScript and use it to connect your front end (UI) to your back end (logic) and your database. These parts will be connected to any service from the internet that you'll need within your product.

Design

Design:
UX, UI, user testing and designâ€”you will need to find the sweet spot between those. On top of that, the website you will build needs to be responsive. After learning HTML and CSS, you will be able to personalise every little detail of your app, across devices and screen sizes.

Deployment

Deployment:
After you make an improvement, it needs to go live. You will learn how to deploy a code change to your domain in minutes. Pushing new code to a server is an integral part of keeping all your changes in sync with your live website.

Design sprint

Week 1

The design sprint is a five-day process for answering critical business questions through design, prototyping, and testing ideas with customers.

During this process, you can shortcut the endless-debate cycle and compress months of time into a single week. Instead of waiting to launch a minimal product to understand if an idea is any good, youâ€™ll get clear data from a realistic prototype.

The sprint gives you a superpower:
You can fast-forward into the future to see your finished product and customer reactions, before making any expensive commitments.

GOAL WEEK 1: Build a high-fidelity prototype and get feedback from real users

SKILLS WEEK 1: User-centered design, wireframing, rapid prototyping and user-testing

User Interface Design

Week 2

User Interface Design is a multi-faceted and challenging task. It is responsible for the transference of a productâ€™s development, research, content and layout into an attractive, guiding and responsive experience for users. It is crucial to any digital interface and for customers a key element to trusting a product/brand.

Besides learning the fundamentals of HTML & CSS, at the core of any fully responsive website is a thorough knowledge of how to troubleshoot HTML and CSS. At the end of this week you will have the knowledge to troubleshoot any front-end issue on your website, which will help you as you move forward to building a completely responsive website that displays properly across all browsers and devices.

GOAL WEEK 2: Pick and customize a web theme that represents your product across devices

SKILLS WEEK 2: HTML & CSS fundamentals, use of dev tools and responsive design

User Experience Design

Week 3

User experience design (UX) is the process of enhancing customer satisfaction and loyalty by improving the usability, ease of use, and pleasure provided in the interaction between the customer and the product.

It is targeted at development of digital products, but the theory and process can be applied to anything. The four stages of UX are: Wireframing, Prototyping, Testing/iteration and Development planning.
On the design sprint week we already tackle wireframing and prototyping therefore now it's the turn for more testing and development planning.

GOAL WEEK 3: Define the interaction between your product and the users

SKILLS WEEK 3: UX design, UX flow and plugin integration

Coding & Mindset

Week 4

Everybody can be a developer if they learn how to think in the right way. That implies applying the scientific method when we face a problem. Implies to slice complex problems into approachable chunks and tackle one by one. Implies taking failure as feedback.
As you can see the developer mindset has similar traits to an entrepreneur mindset. We will see the differences too.

We will learn by doing and we learn to learn. We will start with command-line, which is key to navigating around your computer. We'll tackle version control using Github and finally we will start coding in a hight level language using the basics of javascript as a starting point.

GOAL WEEK 4: Start to think as a developer

SKILLS WEEK 4: Learn how to learn, scientific method in coding, debugging and javascript basics

JavaScript Frontend

Week 5

We will add a basic understanding of the Javascript data structures to your toolkit. By the end of the week, youâ€™ll know enough to start writing some useful programs.

You will code all this on the browser, because, without web browsers, there would be no JavaScript.
And you will realize that:
"The browser is a really hostile programming environment." - Douglas Crockford
But you will learn the concepts(DOM) and tools(jQuery), to deal with this complexity.

GOAL WEEK 5: Add interactivity to your website

SKILLS WEEK 5:JS data structures and functions, JS and the browser, the DOM and jQuery,

Cloud possibilities

Week 6

Depending on your idea, you will need to connect to different online services(the Cloud) to provide an unique product to your users. Maybe you want to offer then SMS, email or voice powered features or provide data or services form facebook, google maps, twitter, youtube, amazon, ...

To do this you will need to learn the voice of the internet(HTTP), the language(JSON) and manage the interaction between yours users, your website and the cloud.

GOAL WEEK 6: Learn how to connect your website to the internet.

SKILLS WEEK 6: JSON, HTTP and event handling

JS Front-End frameworks

Week 9

Javascript frontend technologies are booming currently. There is so much choice and innovation going on. Therefore we will start answering key questions as:
What are the most important technologies to know right now? Where should you invest your time to get the most benefit?

And we will end up selecting the framework that makes more sense to your app taking into consideration factors as: learning curve, productivity, documentation and capabilities.

GOAL WEEK 9: Implement your frontend ecosystem in your application.

SKILLS WEEK 9:SPAs, JS frontend frameworks ,tooling, dependencies and js patterns

Backend

Week 7

Now is the turn to implement the logic of your application. The backend usually consists of two parts: an application, and a database.

Application: Node.js is a simple and powerful tool for backend development. When combined with express, you can create lightweight, fast, scalable APIs quickly and simply.

Database: MongoDB is a very popular open source database. You will learn the essential skills to start using MongoDB to power your software application.

GOAL WEEK 7: Create the backend of your application.

SKILLS WEEK 7: Node.js ecosystem, databases and Mongodb

Connecting to the cloud

Week 8

We will implement a Express server with the goal of serving truly RESTful services using Node.js, Mongoose, and MongoDB. We will implement all of the RESTful verbs to get, add, and update data from our service.
Hooking up your app to the previously selected API'S that will define your specific product.

GOAL WEEK 8: Connecting your app to the cloud

SKILLS WEEK 8: REST, Express.js and API's

MVP live

Week 12

You have come a long way. It's time for the final sprint. With a focus to get all ready for the last day, when you will deliver your elevator pitch showing your MVP.

We will allocate time in this week, to disscuss the future Roadmap of your product with your tutor, interchanging ideas, feedback and getting feedback. With the advice we will share our experiencie not just in tech but in business. The idea is to help you with the next steps you should take after the course.

GOAL WEEK 12: Your MVP needs to be finish for the last day, pitch day

SKILLS WEEK 12: Roadmap planning and elevator pitch

Agile Methodology

Week 10

Youâ€™ll learn how to maximize your daily effort on the tasks that matter the most, better understand how much you can really finish during the day, and create a sustainable cadence in which your work will fit. And, youâ€™ll learn to do this, all while building a great product of your very own.

Agile software development with a focus on effective Scrum and proven team practices. You'll learn how these methodologies affect planning and project management, and programmer practices, which address how to work with the code in an agile way.

GOAL WEEK 10: Apply agile software development to a team of one

SKILLS WEEK 10: Agile, user stories, estimation and scrum

One Week Sprint

Week 11

This is the no theory week. You will just code, share progress and get feedback from your coleagues and tutor. We will continue with agile ceremonies, SCRUM and daily stand up, where each individual shares progress, current task and blockers. Getting support from the tutor on the blockers.

You will understand why estimation is so important for your progress after this course, and you will understand why is so challenging even for seasoned developers.

GOAL WEEK 11: Learn how to estimate how much you can get done in a week

SKILLS WEEK 11: A week in the life of a developer
