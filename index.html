About us

We want to use learning to include everybody in the digital revolution.
Our evening courses will provide you with an environment of likeminded people, that will help you to keep motivation and build the right habits.
We will provide you with the tutoring, knowledge and skills to transform and idea into a digital product.

You will go to our lab from 6:00-8:30 from Monday to Tuesday, for 12 weeks.
In return, you will end up with a working MVP and all the knowledge and skills to transform any idea into a digital product and impress potential investors.

The Skills you will learn

Architecture

Architecture:
You have an idea in mind. Now to transform that idea into a product you will need to come up with a system architectural design. You will learn the thinking process to come up with your own MVP.

Development

Development:
You will transform your architecture design into code. You will learn javascript and use it to connect your frontend(UI) to your backend(Logic) and your database. These parts will be connected to any service from the internet you'll need within your product.

Design

Design:
UX, UI, user testing and design, you will need to find the sweet spot between those. On top of that, the website you will build needs to be responsive. After learning HTML & CSS you will be able to personalize every little details of your app, accross devices and screen sizes.

Deployment

Deployment:
After you make an improvement this needs to go live. You will learn how to deploy a code change to your domain in minutes. Pushing new code to a server is an integral part to keep all your changes in sync with your live website.

Design sprint

Week 1

The design sprint is a five-day process for answering critical business questions through design, prototyping, and testing ideas with customers.

With this process, you can shortcut the endless-debate cycle and compress months of time into a single week. Instead of waiting to launch a minimal product to understand if an idea is any good, you’ll get clear data from a realistic prototype.

The sprint gives you a superpower:
You can fast-forward into the future to see your finished product and customer reactions, before making any expensive commitments.

GOAL WEEK 1: Build a high-fidelity prototype and get feedback for real users

SKILLS WEEK 1: User-centered design, wireframing, rapid prototyping and user testing

User Interface Design

Week 2

User Interface Design is a multi-faceted and challenging task. It is responsible for the transference of a product’s development, research, content and layout into an attractive, guiding and responsive experience for users. It is crucial to any digital interface and for customers a key element to trusting a product/brand.

Besides learning the fundamentals of HTML & CSS, at the core of any fully responsive website is a thorough knowledge of how to troubleshoot HTML and CSS. At the end of this week you will have the knowledge to troubleshoot any front-end issue on your website, which will help you as you move forward to building a completely responsive website that displays properly across all browsers and devices.

GOAL WEEK 2: Pick and customize a web theme that represents your product across devices

SKILLS WEEK 2: HTML & CSS fundamentals, use of dev tools and responsive design

User Experience Design

Week 3

User experience design (UX) is the process of enhancing customer satisfaction and loyalty by improving the usability, ease of use, and pleasure provided in the interaction between the customer and the product.

It is targeted at development of digital products, but the theory and process can be applied to anything. The four stages of UX are: Wireframing, Prototyping, Testing/iteration and Development planning.
On the design sprint week we already tackle wireframing and prototyping therefore now it's the turn for more testing and development planning.

GOAL WEEK 3: Define the interaction between your product and the users

SKILLS WEEK 3: UX design, UX flow and plugin integration

Coding & Mindset

Week 4

Everybody can be a developer if they learn how to think in the right way. That implies applying the scientific method when we face a problem. Implies to slice complex problems into approachable chunks and tackle one by one. Implies taking failure as feedback.
As you can see the developer mindset has similar traits to an entrepreneur mindset. We will see the differences too.

We will learn by doing and we learn to learn. We will start with command-line, which is key to navigating around your computer. We'll tackle version control using Github and finally we will start coding in a hight level language using the basics of javascript as a starting point.

GOAL WEEK 4: Start to think as a developer

SKILLS WEEK 4: Learn how to learn programming, scientific method in coding, debugging and javascript basics.

Faq

- Again, what is this about?

Evening course, Monday to Thrusday, where you will start with an idea on day one, and you end up with a product built by yourself at the end of the 12 weeks. But not just the product, the skills to transform ideas into new digital products.

- What's our definition of a non technical person?

A non-technical person has only the vision of what they want created, and the technical person brings that vision to life by actually doing it, with their own ideas and creativity.
We don't expect any familiriaty with code or product development. According to this definition, you will start as non-technical, and you will become a technical person at the end of the course.

- Why offline?

If you find a similar course online, you should give it a go. Probaly it's cheaper and you can do it at your own pace.
Now if you find, that keeping motivation is difficult, that you never get things done, due to lack of momentun, that you would like to be around likeminded people, that accountability and tutor time will help you to work on the edge of your potential then you just come to the right place.

- How do I know if my idea can become a product?

Send us an email describing your product, and we will come back to you with technical feedback(feasiblity, architecture, scope) and an assestment on the convinience of taking our course.

- What if I share my idea and then you steal it?

Read this post, and you will see that sharing your idea is not only save, but recomended. If you are still not convinced, we are happy to sign a non-disclosure agreement.

- What is a MVP?

A well designed MVP will do just this: It will focus on presenting customers with a solution to the pain that you have identified.
Customers often don’t know what they want, therefore sometimes the only way to prove demand is to build an MVP.

- The definition of MVP is a bit generic, can you be more specific?

There are several types of MVPs. We garantee a single featured MVP, leaving the door open to additional features.

- Why evenings?

Sometimes an entrepreneur needs to risk their day job to then find out, that they are not being productive enough or they are not motivated enough. We don't think that's the best approach. An evening course, allows you to postpone the resignation letter moment, minimizing the risk.

- Why four days a week?

Momentun. You are going to be expose to an incredible amount of new concepts, ideas, paradigms, and to asssimilate that in 12 weeks, you need to dedicate continuous effort to them.
After all, it does not matter how slowly you go as long as you do not stop. Well, that's partially true at goDigital, you won't stop during 12 weeks, but we will go fast.

- What if I can just go 3 days a week?

Then you shouldn't take the course. Try to accomodate your responsabilities to fit 4 days a week, and apply for a future cohort.

- What if I miss some days?

That's alright, the program assumes you will miss 2 days a month but not more.

- Do you guarantee my success?

If you are going to put the time, and accept the idea, that failure is feedback, you will have a working MVP at the end of the course. You will become a technical person too.
I just assume you are familiar with the use of a computer and you are able to use logical thinking to apply the scientific method.

- Can you tell me a bit more about your coaching style?

Our coaching style is based in several principles that lead to success when learning software engineering. We have seen these patterns appear again and again: Momemtun, 80/20 rule(Pareto principle), scientific method, build tolerance to failure, spaced repetition session, theory/practice ratio(theory time 10%, practice time 90%), pomodore technique avoiding context switching, retention rate, loads of stackoverflow merkat method(tutor) and more....

- How is a day at go digital?

18:00-18:10 Code warm up
18:10-18:25 Spaced repetition session
18:30-18:40 Theory
18:45-19:10 1st daily pomodoro
19:15-19:45 2nd daily pomodoro
19:50-20:05 Individual coaching time
20:05 -20:30 3rd daily pomodoro

At the end of the day expect, step closer to your MVP, new concepts added to your skill set.

- How many people per tutor? Always same tutor?

8 people per group, with the same tutor exclusively dedicated to them during the 12 weeks.
You will stay with the same 8 people within your group and with the same tutor. All of you will share the hights & lows.
Tutors, unfortunately will get sick too. In those days, a different tutor will assist the group.

- Do I need to work at home

Just 4-5 hours on the weekend. The idea is trying to minimize as much as posible the work at home.
The course is already quite intense, and we are aware life gets in the middle too therefore workload outsite of the Lab will be minimized.

- I saw you use javascript, why?

Javascript is used everywhere. Only language you can use in the browser and to right the logic in the backend. You don't want to learn an extra language on top of all the contents of the program.
Plenty of resources(documentation, frameworks, apis, ...)
Most popular technology for developers.

- Technologies I can use?

Once you send us your idea, we will provide a list of suggestions that match your needs, e.g. sms, automated phone calls --> Twilio, email --> SendGrid, ...

- Will I learn to code in this course?

Yes. Not only that, you will adquire the mindset of a developer, that means you will know how to learn a new technology from scratch and transform your ideas into something real.

- Can I get a job as a software engineer after this?

No, but...
There are levels thatsoftware companies

1500£ for the 12 week program.

- Can I pay you with equity?

We don't accept equity, because again, the success of a startup depends a lot on the founder, not on the idea.
And we won't know you well until the end of the course. At that point, if it makes sense for both parts to have some kind of collaboration, let's do it.

Central London, Old Street

This is the 1st addition of the course and therefore, just 8 entrepreneurs will be accepted. No gimmick, I'm afraid.

- How do I get a spot on the program?

1st Step, send the idea: We need to access your product is technically doable, and manage your expectations about the features you will get done by the end of the program.
2nd Step, logical-analytical tests: We will send you online logic tests, to measure you logical and analytical skills. You may be rejected initially on those, but don't give up, we will send you resources to get ready, in case you want to take them again. After all, we think that persistence is what will make you successful.
3rd Step, personal interview: We want to know about you a bit more and have the opportunity to answer some of the questions you might have about the program. 4th Step, reservation payment --> to guarante your spot.
